## 1. THE HOSPITALIZATION FRAMEWORK (foundational doctrine)

### Primary houses for hospitalization

- **H12** — confinement, hospitalization, foreign-to-home setting,
  hidden burden (the PRIMARY hospitalization house)
- **H6** — disease itself (the reason for hospitalization)
- **H8** — sudden intervention, surgery, transformation through illness
- **H4** — home (the place one is RETURNING to upon discharge)

### Primary recovery houses (the trinity)

- **H1** — self / body / vitality returns
- **H5** — 12th from H6 → "loss of disease" / end of illness
- **H11** — fulfillment of recovery desire / gain of health

### The structural cycle

```
DISEASE (H6) → ADMISSION (H12) → INTERVENTION (H8 if surgery, H6 ongoing)
            → RECOVERY (H1 + H5 + H11) → DISCHARGE (H4 + return home)
```

The KP astrologer maps the chart against this cycle:
- Where is the current dasha in this cycle?
- When does the dasha shift from disease/intervention houses to
  recovery houses?
- That shift = the structural discharge window.

## 2. ADMISSION SIGNATURE — WHEN HOSPITALIZATION IS STRUCTURALLY ACTIVE

### The standard admission pattern

H12 + H6 active in current AD:
- H6 sub lord signifies H12 = disease leads to hospitalization
- H12 sub lord signifies H6 + H8 = active illness requiring confinement
- Saturn or Mars dasha amplifies (Saturn = chronic stay; Mars = acute/surgical)

### Emergency vs scheduled admission

**Emergency admission**:
- H8 (sudden) prominently active
- Mars + Rahu involvement (sudden + unconventional / accident)
- Quick-firing dasha (fast planet AD/PAD)
- Pattern D2 risk: events fire before native expected

**Scheduled admission** (elective surgery, planned treatment):
- H8 active but with Saturn modifier (planned + structured)
- Mercury favorable (medical documentation, planning)
- Jupiter favorable (intentional + considered intervention)
- Typically read alongside elective surgery doctrine in health_detailed.md §6

## 3. ICU / CRITICAL CARE — TIER 3 ESCALATION

⚠ **When ICU / critical care / coma is mentioned in the question,
apply TIER 3 ABSOLUTE framing per sensitivity_tiers.md.**

### Structural ICU signature

- H12 + H8 + Mars/Saturn affliction simultaneously
- H1 sub lord signifying H12 alongside Maraka or Badhaka activation
- Recovery houses (H1/H5/H11) WEAK or blocked in current AD
- Multiple malefic planets converging on H8 + H12

### What this signal MEANS and does NOT mean

**MEANS**:
- The chart shows structural support for the critical-care context
  the family is experiencing
- The dasha shift to recovery houses tells the structural recovery
  window
- The astrologer can identify favorable counter-signals + treatment
  windows even in critical context

**DOES NOT MEAN**:
- The patient will not survive (RULE 15 absolute — never predict death)
- A specific date when life support should be withdrawn
- That medical intervention is futile

### ICU question framing protocol

For "my [family member] is in ICU — will they make it?":

1. Prepend Tier 3 NOTE (per sensitivity_tiers.md §3)
2. Apply Bhavat Bhavam if reading via family member's chart
   (confidence floor ~70%)
3. Read structural cycle (where in disease → recovery cycle?)
4. Identify the dasha shift point (when H1/H5/H11 activate)
5. Cite favorable counter-signals (Jupiter aspects, strong H1 lord,
   Moon-Jupiter alignment)
6. Recommend: tertiary specialty center if not already there + ICU
   liaison + family presence + spiritual support
7. Crisis / family-support resources cited
8. Close on hope branch + concrete next steps
9. NEVER name a death date or "won't make it" verdict

## 4. DISCHARGE TIMING — THE H1+H5+H11 + H4 FRAMEWORK

### The discharge structural signature

Per KP doctrine:

**Discharge promised when**:
- H4 sub lord (residential area / home) signifies H3 (movement) AND H1
  (self) — native is structurally returning home
- H1 sub lord favorable (vitality returns)
- H5 sub lord active (end-of-disease via 12th-from-H6 logic)
- H11 sub lord favorable (recovery-fulfillment)
- AD or upcoming PAD shifts from H6/H8/H12 dominance to H1/H5/H11/H4

### Specific timing procedure

Step 1: Identify the current AD's house focus
- If H6/H8/H12 dominant → illness continuing
- If H1/H5/H11 starting to activate → discharge approaching
- If H4 lord active → return home structurally indicated

Step 2: Scan upcoming PADs within current AD
- Find first PAD where H1 lord, H4 lord, OR H11 lord activates
- That PAD = the structural discharge window

Step 3: Refine with planetary character
- Mercury favorable PAD → discharge paperwork smooth + quick
- Jupiter favorable PAD → favorable treatment response, discharged
  with good prognosis
- Venus favorable PAD → comfortable transition home
- Saturn PAD → discharge possibly delayed by paperwork/insurance
- Mars PAD → discharge may be premature; risk of re-admission

Step 4: Cross-check with Ruling Planets at question moment
- If RPs include H1/H4/H11 lord = high confidence on the structural window
- If RPs include H6/H8/H12 lord = window still ahead

### Discharge planet character

| Dasha planet | Discharge character |
|---|---|
| Sun | Authority-cleared discharge (e.g., consultant signs off); prestige-aligned |
| Moon | Sentiment-driven (family pressure for discharge); home-comfort focus |
| Mars | Fast / sudden discharge; risk of premature exit |
| Mercury | Smooth paperwork; multiple medication/follow-up instructions |
| Jupiter | Discharge with positive prognosis; full recovery indicated |
| Venus | Comfortable discharge; comfort-focused home setup |
| Saturn | Slow / delayed discharge; structured plan + insurance complexity |
| Rahu | Unconventional discharge route (transfer / against medical advice / foreign) |
| Ketu | Sudden release (sometimes good — patient recovers faster than expected) |

## 5. POST-DISCHARGE HOME RECOVERY

### Recovery at home framework

After discharge, recovery continues. The KP doctrine:

- **H1 sub lord** in upcoming PADs → ongoing vitality strengthening
- **H4 sub lord** favorable → home environment supportive
- **H5 sub lord** favorable → emotional/intellectual healing
- **H11 sub lord** favorable → social support active
- **Jupiter aspects** during recovery period → divine grace / good
  prognosis maintained

### Recovery milestone timing

| Window | Approximate timing | Astrological marker |
|---|---|---|
| First week | PAD level | H1 sub lord first activation post-discharge |
| First month | Sookshma-level firing within PAD | Sun or Jupiter monthly transit favorable |
| First 3 months | 2-3 PADs | Recovery dasha consolidation |
| First year | AD level | Annual transit return + dasha review |
| Full recovery | Multi-AD horizon | Chronic/acute distinction per health.txt §8 |

### Re-admission risk

Watch for:
- Premature discharge (Mars PAD prematurely activating)
- H6 sub lord re-activation in PAD shortly after discharge
- H8 + H12 active again → relapse / re-admission likely
- Cross-link to chronic vs acute framework in health_detailed.md §8

### Lifestyle support recommendations (by recovery-favorable planet)

- **Jupiter favorable** → spiritual practice + dharmic activities
  support recovery (meditation, satsang, study of meaning)
- **Mercury favorable** → cognitive engagement (reading, light work,
  hobbies that require thinking)
- **Venus favorable** → social + creative recovery (music, art,
  light social engagement)
- **Sun favorable** → daylight exposure + light physical activity
- **Moon favorable** → emotional connection + family time
- **Mars favorable** → graduated physical rehabilitation, exercise
- **Saturn favorable** → structured routine, patience-based recovery
- **Rahu favorable** → unconventional therapies that work for this native
- **Ketu favorable** → meditation, withdrawal, deep rest

## 6. SURGERY-RELATED HOSPITALIZATION

Cross-link to `health_detailed.md` §6 (Surgery Indicators) and
`child_health.md` §6 (if pediatric).

### Surgery-hospitalization specific timing

- Pre-surgery admission: 1-3 days, typically H12 active
- Surgery itself: H8 active + Mars favorable for clean intervention
- Post-surgery ICU/recovery: H12 + H1 (transitioning)
- Step-down ward: H1 + H4 (preparing return)
- Discharge: per §4 framework

### Surgery success → fast discharge

- H8 sub lord signifies H11 cleanly → surgery successful → faster
  discharge
- H1 + H5 + H11 firing in immediate post-op PAD = quick recovery

### Surgery complications → extended hospitalization

- H8 sub lord signifies H8 + H12 alone (no H11) → complications
- H12 prolonged activation post-surgery
- Re-operation risk if Mars+Saturn affliction continues

NEVER frame psychiatric admission as shame, failure, or weakness.
NEVER recommend stopping psychiatric medication.

## 8. REPEAT / FREQUENT HOSPITALIZATION PATTERNS

When a native asks "why am I always in the hospital" or "is this going
to keep happening":

### Chronic illness signature (per health_detailed.md §8 Chronic vs Acute)

- H8 + H12 chronically active across multiple ADs
- Saturn dominance in health-related chains
- Multi-year dasha pattern activating disease houses repeatedly
- This is the CHRONIC pattern — frame as ongoing management

### Lifestyle-correlation pattern

When repeat hospitalization correlates with specific lifestyle factors:
- H2 affliction (diet/throat-mouth consumption)
- H6 chronic (work-stress related)
- H12 + Rahu/Ketu (sleep disorders / substance-related)

Recommend lifestyle changes aligned with chart-supportive timing
(Jupiter favorable window for habit-change initiation, etc.)

### Severe chronic-illness framing

Apply longevity.md cross-reference if chronic illness raises longevity
concerns. Apply Tier 3 framing if the question contains anxiety
about "will this kill me eventually" language.

## 10. PROTECTIVE FRAMING (TIER 2 default / TIER 3 escalators)

### Standard Tier 2 framing (most hospitalization questions)

**(a) Acknowledge the family's burden**:
> "Hospitalization is hard for the whole family — the patient and
> everyone walking with them through it. The chart shows the
> structural backdrop against which this happens."

**(b) Confidence floor if Bhavat Bhavam**:
> "Reading via your chart gives ~70% confidence on [family member]'s
> hospitalization context. Their own birth data would tighten the
> discharge-window precision."

**(c) Cycle stage stated explicitly**:
> "Per the disease→admission→intervention→recovery→discharge cycle,
> your chart shows you currently in the [X] stage. The shift to
> [Y stage] is structurally supported in [specific dasha window]."

**(d) Discharge window with calendar dates**:
> "The structural discharge window opens [specific PAD calendar dates].
> Actual discharge depends on medical clearance, but the chart
> supports this timing."

**(e) Recovery support recommended**:
> "Post-discharge, the structural recovery pattern is supported by
> [specific lifestyle / family / professional recommendations
> aligned with planetary character]."

**(f) Close on hope + agency**:
> "Hospitalization is a chapter, not the whole story. The chart's
> recovery chapter is structurally accessible — the medical team +
> family + your own resilience write it."

### Tier 3 escalation (ICU / critical care / coma / life-support)

ALL of §3 ICU protocol above + sensitivity_tiers.md §3 Tier 3
ABSOLUTE framing applies. Crisis resources UPFRONT.

## 13. WORKED EXAMPLES

### Example 1: "My father had a heart attack — when will he be discharged?"

**Approach**:
1. Tier 2 NOTE (escalate to Tier 3 if ICU/coma involved)
2. Bhavat Bhavam from native's H9 = father's H1
3. Read father's structural cycle stage (currently H8 active = intervention)
4. Identify next PAD where father's H1+H5+H11+H4 starts firing
5. State discharge window (e.g., "structural discharge window opens
   approximately [date], assuming medical clearance")
6. Recommend: post-discharge cardiac rehab + lifestyle support
   (specific to father's Moon condition)
7. Close on family agency + hope

### Example 2: "I've been in psychiatric hospital for 3 weeks — when will I go home?"

**Approach**:
1. Tier 2 NOTE with mental_health.md cross-reference
2. Read H12 → H1+H4 transition timing
3. Cite Moon condition rebuilding + Jupiter favorable transit
4. Specific PAD when discharge structurally supported
5. CRITICAL: emphasize that medication adherence + therapy continuity
   post-discharge are structurally indicated
6. Validate the struggle without minimizing
7. Close on the next chapter being structurally accessible

### Example 3: "My wife is in ICU after car accident — will she recover?"

**Approach**:
1. **Tier 3 NOTE prepended + crisis family resources cited**
2. Bhavat Bhavam from native's H7 = wife's H1
3. Read structural cycle (ICU = H12 + H8 dominant)
4. Identify favorable counter-signals (Jupiter aspect, H1 lord strength)
5. Show both branches (recovery branch + adverse branch)
6. Recommend: tertiary specialty trauma center if not already there +
   family liaison + chaplain/spiritual support
7. Close on hope + agency + family love
8. Crisis resources cited again

### Example 4: "I keep getting admitted every few months — why?"

**Approach**:
1. Tier 2 NOTE
2. Read chronic pattern (H6+H12 chronic activation per §8)
3. Identify the dasha pattern amplifying chronicity
4. Cite the next dasha shift where structural ease arrives
5. Cross-link to chronic disease management framing in health.txt §8
6. Lifestyle + treatment continuity recommendations
7. Acknowledge the exhaustion of repeated hospitalization
8. Close on the structural ease window + agency

### Example 5: "Should we discharge my mother to home or move to a nursing facility?"

**Approach**: Compound question — apply sensitivity_tiers.md §11
compound Tier-3 protocol:
1. Tier 3 NOTE (elderly + decision-support frame)
2. Decompose: (a) mother's recovery capacity, (b) home care vs facility
   structural support, (c) family caregiver capacity, (d) financial
   capacity
3. Each axis read independently
4. Three branches (home / facility / hybrid)
5. NOT a verdict — decision-support framing
6. Recommend: geriatric care consultation + family meeting + financial
   review
7. Close on family agency + concrete next steps
