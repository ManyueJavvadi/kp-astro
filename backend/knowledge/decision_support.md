# Decision Support — "Should I Do X" Framework

**Purpose**: Meta-topic KB for decision-support questions. Most KP
questions are predictive ("when will X happen") or diagnostic ("why
is X happening"). Decision-support questions are different — they
ask "should I do X" and require a STRUCTURED decision-support
methodology, not a verdict.

**Source**: RULE 29 (Decision Support + Conflict Flags) + classical
KP doctrine on alternative-path analysis + decision-science overlay.

**Sensitivity tier**: Inherits from underlying topic. A "should I
quit my job for business" question = Tier 2 (career + business);
"should I divorce my spouse" = Tier 2-3 (marriage + grief); "should
we proceed with child's surgery" = Tier 3 ABSOLUTE compound.

**Engine support**: Topic `decision` (canonical, PR B5.0a) + aliases
(`should_i`, `good_idea`, `right_choice`, `choose_between`,
`decision_help`, `what_to_do`, `comparison`) route here via PR B5.5.

## 1. THE FOUNDATIONAL DOCTRINE

### What makes a question a "decision-support" question

A decision-support question:
1. Frames as "should I" / "should we" / "is it good to" / "what
   should I do"
2. Implicitly or explicitly contains 2+ options
3. The client is the AGENT making the choice
4. The chart can inform but cannot decide for them

Examples:
- "Should I quit my job to start a business?" (career + business + decision)
- "Should I marry this person?" (marriage + decision)
- "Should I take this loan or save more first?" (wealth + decision)
- "Should I do this pilgrimage now or wait?" (pilgrimage + decision)
- "Should I file divorce or try to reconcile?" (divorce + decision)

### What KP CAN offer

- Structural support assessment for each option
- Timing windows favoring each path
- Risk/cost identification for each option
- Personal-pattern recognition (which option fits the chart's character)

### What KP CANNOT offer

- THE decision (the agent decides)
- A "correct" answer (often both options are valid in different ways)
- Removal of risk (every choice carries some risk)

### The astrologer's role in decision support

> "I'll help you see the structural support each path has, the
> timing windows, the risk patterns. The decision itself stays with
> you — and your family, your advisors, your own discernment."

NEVER deliver decision-support as verdict. ALWAYS as MULTI-BRANCH
ANALYSIS with the client making the call.

## 2. RULE 29 — THE FOUNDATIONAL FRAMEWORK

(Already in `get_system_prompt()` as RULE 29 — Decision Support +
Conflict Flags)

Per RULE 29, decision-support questions follow this structure:

### Step 1: Decompose the question

Identify each axis the decision touches:
- Underlying topic(s) (career / business / marriage / etc.)
- Resources at stake (time / money / relationships)
- Reversibility (can the decision be undone?)
- Stakeholders (just self, or family / community?)

### Step 2: Read structural support for each path

For EACH option:
- What does the chart say about this path's natural fit?
- What's the timing window for this path?
- What's the structural sustainability?
- What's the cost / risk pattern?

### Step 3: Identify conflicts and alignments

- Which option does the chart's character favor (long-arc planetary
  inclination)?
- Which option does the current dasha favor (current-period
  inclination)?
- Are these aligned (chart character + current dasha agree) or in
  conflict (chart character favors A, current dasha favors B)?

### Step 4: Present multi-branch analysis

NEVER reduce to a verdict. Present:
- BRANCH A: full structural reading
- BRANCH B: full structural reading
- (If applicable) BRANCH C: hybrid / third option
- Synthesis: not "do A" or "do B" but "here's how to weigh each"

### Step 5: Recommend external consultation

The chart is ONE input. Recommend:
- Domain-specific professional (lawyer / financial planner / doctor)
- Family discussion
- Mentor / spiritual counselor
- Personal reflection time

## 3. THE 7-STEP DECISION-SUPPORT PROTOCOL

This expands RULE 29 into a concrete output protocol:

### Step 1 — Mirror the question back

> "You're asking whether to [option A] or [option B] — am I reading
> this correctly?"

This clarifies the actual decision being made (often there's a deeper
question underneath).

### Step 2 — Acknowledge the weight

> "This is a meaningful decision because [stakes — time / money /
> family / lifestyle]. The chart shows the structural backdrop;
> the choice is yours."

### Step 3 — Read each path structurally

For BRANCH A (option 1):
- Underlying topic doctrine applied (career.md / marriage.txt / etc.)
- Structural support: STRONG / MODERATE / WEAK
- Timing window
- Risk pattern
- Counter-signals to monitor

For BRANCH B (option 2):
- Same structural read

(If applicable) BRANCH C (hybrid / third path):
- Some decisions have a creative third option

### Step 4 — Identify the chart's character preference (long-arc)

> "Looking at your chart's long-arc character (Saturn dasha pattern,
> dominant planets, lagna lord), the inclination is toward [path A
> / path B / both viable]."

This is the BIRTH-CHART personality preference, independent of
current period.

### Step 5 — Identify the current dasha's preference (short-arc)

> "Looking at your current dasha (MD/AD/PAD), the period favors
> [path A timing window / path B timing window]."

This is the CURRENT period's preference.

### Step 6 — Resolve character + current-period alignment

- **Both agree** (character + current both favor A): structural
  confidence in path A
- **Conflict** (character favors A, current favors B): consider WAITING
  for character-aligned period, OR take the current-period path with
  awareness it's against long-arc character
- **Both weak**: this isn't a structural-promised decision — both
  paths are valid; the choice is more values-based than structural

### Step 7 — Recommend external + close on agency

> "Recommended consultations alongside this reading:
>   [domain-specific professionals]
>   [family / community]
>   [personal reflection / spiritual practice]
>
> The chart shows the structural backdrop. The decision is yours,
> with these inputs."

## 5. WHEN BOTH OPTIONS ARE STRUCTURALLY VIABLE

Many real decisions have both options structurally supported. The
question becomes "what's the values-aligned choice" rather than
"what's the chart-promised choice."

### Framing

> "Per the structural reading, both [option A] and [option B] have
> structural support in your chart. The chart shows STRUCTURAL
> POSSIBILITY for both; the choice is more about your values, your
> family's needs, and what you feel called to.
>
> Some clients in your structural position have chosen [option A]
> with these outcomes; others have chosen [option B] with these
> outcomes. Both are valid life-paths.
>
> Recommended decision-aid process:
>   1. Sleep on it (the chart's signals work alongside intuition)
>   2. Discuss with [trusted family / mentor]
>   3. Spend time in a setting that represents each path (visit
>      the workplace / business space; visit the city you're
>      considering moving to)
>   4. Notice your body's response — sometimes the body knows
>      before the mind"

This is a critical decision-support style — NOT every question
has a chart-promised answer.

---

## 6. WHEN ONE OPTION IS STRUCTURALLY STRONGER

When the chart clearly favors one path:

### Framing

> "Per the structural reading, [option A] has stronger structural
> support: [specific factors]. The current dasha favors this path's
> activation in [window]. The chart's character (long-arc personality)
> also leans this direction.
>
> [Option B] has [some support / mostly denial] — meaning the chart's
> structural backdrop is less aligned with this choice.
>
> This doesn't mean [option B] is 'wrong' — values, circumstances,
> and your own discernment matter. It means the structural support
> from the chart is concentrated on [option A]."

Allow the client to override this — they may have non-chart reasons
to choose B.

---

## 7. WHEN BOTH OPTIONS ARE STRUCTURALLY WEAK

Less common — but real. The chart shows neither path has strong
support in current window.

### Framing

> "Per the structural reading, neither [option A] nor [option B] has
> strong structural support in your current period. This often means:
>   - The current period favors WAITING (next favorable AD: [X])
>   - The current period favors a THIRD path you haven't named
>   - The chart's character is in transition — clarity will come in
>     [Y window]
>
> Recommended:
>   - Pause major decisions until [specific period]
>   - Use this period for preparation / skill-building / inner work
>   - Consult [domain-specific professional + spiritual mentor] for
>     additional perspective"

## 8. CONFLICT DETECTION (RULE 29 — flag for AI)

Some decisions have COMPETING CHART SIGNALS — career favorable but
marriage strained; business strong but health vulnerable. RULE 29
requires flagging these:

### Sample conflict flag

> "FLAG: your current chart shows competing signals:
>   - [Career signal] strongly favors [option A]
>   - [Health signal] cautions against the stress of [option A]
>
> This is a structural CONFLICT — both signals are real. Resolution
> typically requires:
>   - Acknowledging both
>   - Identifying which can be modulated (e.g., health stress can be
>     managed; career-window cannot wait)
>   - Building support structures for the stressed dimension"

## 9. DECISION-PARALYSIS HANDLING

Some clients are in chronic decision-paralysis (Mercury + Saturn
afflicted in their chart). KP doctrine:

### Signature

- Mercury afflicted (analytical paralysis)
- Saturn in H1 / H5 (over-deliberation)
- H7 sub lord weak (can't choose between options)
- Often: anxiety condition (cross-link to `mental_health.md` §2.2)

### Framing

> "Your chart shows tendency for prolonged deliberation. This is
> structural — your nature is to weigh carefully. The cost is
> sometimes paralysis.
>
> For this decision:
>   - The structural reading is [option A / option B / either].
>   - Recommendation: set a DECISION DEADLINE (e.g., 2 weeks from
>     today)
>   - Use the deliberation period intentionally — gather inputs,
>     consult, reflect
>   - At deadline, COMMIT to the path that has more structural
>     support + your intuition's pull
>   - Once committed, don't re-litigate"

### Therapy referral

For chronic decision-paralysis affecting life function, cross-link
to `mental_health.md` §2.2 (anxiety / OCD).

## 10. THE "MY [FAMILY MEMBER] WANTS ME TO DO X" CASE

Family pressure decisions are common, especially in Indian context:
"My parents want me to marry this person" / "My spouse wants us to
move abroad" / "My family wants me to take over the family business."

### Approach

1. Honor BOTH the client's autonomy + the family's care
2. Read structural support for the family-suggested path
3. Read structural support for the client's preferred path (if
   different)
4. Identify alignment OR conflict
5. Recommend family communication strategies
6. Cross-link to `parents_family.md` if family-conflict pattern
   strong

### Critical framing

> "The chart shows the structural support for [family-suggested
> path]: [favorable / mixed / weak]. The chart also shows your
> chart's character preference: [path A / B].
>
> When family preference aligns with your chart character →
> structural confidence + family harmony.
>
> When they DIFFER → this is a conversation, not a verdict. The
> family's care is real; your own life-direction is also real. KP
> doesn't take sides — it helps each side see the structural
> backdrop."

## 12. ABSOLUTE PROHIBITIONS

1. ❌ Never deliver decision-support as a verdict ("you should do A")
2. ❌ Never reduce multi-branch to a single recommendation
3. ❌ Never override the client's autonomy
4. ❌ Never pressure based on family / cultural expectations
5. ❌ Never use decision-support to manipulate the client toward
   astrologer's preference
6. ❌ Never charge premium for "the right decision"

### Permissions

1. ✅ Mirror the question + acknowledge weight
2. ✅ Read each path structurally
3. ✅ Identify character vs current-dasha alignment
4. ✅ Flag conflicts honestly (RULE 29)
5. ✅ Recommend external professional consultation
6. ✅ Acknowledge when both paths are valid
7. ✅ Recommend pause if no path has strong support

## 14. WORKED EXAMPLE — CANONICAL COMPOUND DECISION

(Cross-link to `sensitivity_tiers.md` §11)

> "We are a couple aged 32 and 30. Our child was born with a serious
> medical condition. The doctors say the child will not live beyond
> a year without surgery. The surgery costs ₹40 lakhs and even with
> surgery the success rate is only 60%. We have only ₹15 lakhs saved.
> Should we proceed?"

This is the canonical 5-axis compound decision codified in
`child_health.md` §7. The 7-step decision-support protocol applied
to this question:

1. Mirror: "You're asking whether to proceed with a ₹40L pediatric
   surgery with 60% success rate, given your ₹15L savings + 1-year
   prognosis without surgery. Am I reading correctly?"
2. Acknowledge: This is among the hardest decisions a family makes.
3-5. Read each branch (proceed-favorable / proceed-adverse / defer-
     accept) via 5-axis compound
6. Synthesis: not a verdict but the chart's contribution to the
   conversation
7. Recommend: pediatric oncology + financial counselor + family
   counselor + spiritual support

The chart doesn't decide. The family decides with their medical
team, financial reality, and their hearts. The chart's contribution
is making the structural backdrop visible.
