SECTION 1: FOUNDATION — WHAT IS KP AND HOW IT WORKS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

KP (Krishnamurti Paddhati) was developed by Prof. K.S. Krishnamurti (1908-1972).
Stellar system of prediction based on Sub Lords — further subdivision of each
nakshatra into 9 unequal parts based on the Vimshottari Dasha proportions.

THE GOLDEN RULE OF KP:
"Planet is the SOURCE. Star-Lord indicates NATURE OF RESULTS.
Sub-Lord is the DECIDING FACTOR — whether the result is favorable or not."

KP uses:
- Placidus house system (semi-arc) — houses may be unequal in span
- Sidereal zodiac with KP New Ayanamsa (SIDM_KRISHNAMURTI_VP291)
- 27 nakshatras, each divided into 9 subs = 249 sub-lord positions
- Vimshottari Dasha for timing (120 year cycle)
- No planet is inherently good or bad — context determines everything

SECTION 2: THE CUSPAL SUB LORD — MOST IMPORTANT RULE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The sub lord of a house cusp determines whether that house's matter fructifies.
This is the PRIMARY GATE. No favorable dasha can override a closed primary gate.

HOW TO READ A CUSP SUB LORD (4-step expansion of significations):
Step 1: Find the sub lord of the relevant cusp.
Step 2: Find ALL houses signified by that sub lord through:
  (a) Houses the sub lord OCCUPIES
  (b) Houses the sub lord OWNS (sign lordship)
  (c) Houses occupied/owned by the sub lord's STAR LORD
  (d) Houses occupied/owned by the star lord of step (c) — the deepest layer
Step 3: The UNION of all houses from steps (a)-(d) is the "signification set" of the CSL.
Step 4: Compare this set against the topic's RELEVANT houses (promise) and DENIAL houses (obstruction).

NOTE ON 3-STEP VS 4-STEP THEORY:
KSK's original Reader uses primarily a 3-step chain (Planet → Star Lord → Sub Lord, with sub lord
as the final decider). The "4-step theory" (extending to "star lord of sub lord") was proposed
by Sunil Gondhalekar post-KSK. Most modern KP software uses the 4-step expansion above to
maximize signification capture. We follow the 4-step expansion in this engine.

PROMISE VERDICT — 5-TIER SCALE (use this exact scale, not binary):

1. STRONGLY PROMISED:
   - CSL is an A-level or B-level significator of multiple relevant houses
   - ZERO denial-house touch
   - Event happens with smoothness, in the natural dasha window

2. PROMISED:
   - CSL signifies AT LEAST ONE relevant house (any A/B/C/D level)
   - Denial-house touch is minimal or absent
   - Event happens, may have minor delays

3. CONDITIONAL (Dual Signification — KSK strict rule applies):
   - CSL signifies BOTH relevant AND denial houses
   - Per KSK Reader: event FIRES during bhuktis of relevant-house significators,
     and is BLOCKED during bhuktis of denial-house significators
   - This is bhukti-level precision, NOT a soft "happens with delay"
   - When stating verdict: enumerate WHICH bhuktis fire vs which block

4. WEAKLY PROMISED:
   - CSL signifies relevant houses only at C-level or D-level (weak)
   - Multiple bhuktis may pass without fruition
   - Event likely only in specific high-confirmation windows (RP overlap)

5. DENIED:
   - CSL has ZERO connection to any relevant house at any level
   - Only signifies denial houses
   - Event will not happen in this lifetime
   - **DENIAL IS RARE** — only declare denial when there is truly zero relevant signification

UPACHAYA / NEGATING HOUSES (CONTEXTUAL — not absolute):
KP recognizes the traditional Upachaya concept (improving houses 1, 2, 3, 6, 10, 11)
as a CONTEXTUAL framework, NOT as an absolute rule. In KP:
  - No house is INHERENTLY good or bad — context determines effect
  - The cusp sub lord's signification of the topic's RELEVANT houses is what matters
  - Upachaya houses are useful when CSL signification is ambiguous as a tiebreaker
  - DO NOT declare an event "denied" simply because the CSL touches "negating" houses 4, 5, 8, 9, 12 —
    those are denial houses ONLY for specific topics (e.g., 5/9 are negation for job, but 5 is RELEVANT for children)
  - Always determine denial houses from the TOPIC, not from the generic Upachaya list

REFERENCE: KSK Reader 1, "no planet is inherently good or bad — house signification context decides".

SECTION 2.5: STAR–SUB HARMONY — THE TENSION BETWEEN LAYERS (CORE KSK INSIGHT)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

KP is NOT a 4-step UNION operation in which any signified house counts
equally. KP is a TENSION between two layers: the Star Lord declares the
nature of the matter, the Sub Lord decides whether it actually fructifies.

THE TWO-LAYER ARCHITECTURE (KSK Reader I, verbatim spirit):
  LAYER 1 — STAR LORD (the WHAT):
    The star lord of a planet (or of a cusp's sub lord) tells you the
    NATURE of the result the planet/cusp will deliver. It answers:
    "What kind of matter does this position promote?"

  LAYER 2 — SUB LORD (the WHETHER):
    The sub lord, sitting INSIDE the star, tells you whether that matter
    will actually be PERMITTED to fructify. It answers:
    "Is the result favorable or denied?"

KSK's exact phrasing: "The Star-Lord indicates the nature of the result.
The Sub-Lord is the deciding factor — whether the result is favorable or
not." (Reader I, Sub Lord chapter.)

WHY 4-STEP UNION ALONE IS WRONG:
A naive 4-step UNION treats all signified houses as additive — "the CSL
signifies 7 AND 1, both touching." That hides the tension. KSK's strict
reading is:
  - If STAR signifies the relevant house but SUB signifies the denial
    house → DENIAL DOMINATES (sub is the deciding layer).
  - If STAR signifies the denial house but SUB signifies the relevant
    house → THE EVENT FIRES with friction (sub overrides star).
  - If BOTH layers signify relevant houses → STRONGLY PROMISED, smooth.
  - If BOTH layers signify denial houses → DENIED.

STAR–SUB HARMONY SCORE (use this when writing predictions):
  HARMONY (++): Both star and sub signify relevant houses → strongest
  ALIGNED (+) : Sub signifies relevant, star is neutral or weak relevant
  TENSION (-) : Star signifies relevant but sub signifies denial → BLOCK
  CONTRA (-) : Star signifies denial but sub signifies relevant → fires
              with friction (event happens, may have side-effects)
  DENIED (--): Both layers signify denial houses

THIS IS WHY DUAL-SIGNIFICATION IS NOT "SOFT DELAY":
When the CSL signifies BOTH relevant + denial houses, do NOT call it a
"delayed" event. KSK strict rule:
  - Identify which signification comes from the STAR layer vs SUB layer
  - Event FIRES in bhuktis of the layer that points to relevant houses
  - Event is BLOCKED in bhuktis of the layer that points to denial houses
This is bhukti-level precision, not vague delay.

WORKED EXAMPLE (marriage, H7 CSL):
  H7 cusp sub lord = Saturn, in star of Mercury, in sub of Mars.
  Saturn (the planet itself) — occupies H10, owns H1 + H2.
  Mercury (Saturn's STAR LORD) — occupies H11, owns H7 + H4.
  Mars (Saturn's SUB LORD) — occupies H1, owns H6 + H11.

  STAR layer (Mercury) signifies: H11, H7, H4 → 7 and 11 are RELEVANT for
    marriage. STAR points to MARRIAGE PROMISED.
  SUB layer (Mars) signifies: H1, H6, H11 → 1 and 6 are DENIAL for
    marriage; 11 is relevant. SUB is mixed but leans denial.

  HARMONY READING: Star strongly relevant, Sub mixed-leans-denial.
  Verdict: TENSION (star says yes, sub blocks). Event possible only when
  the running bhukti emphasises the 11 thread of the sub (Mars's H11
  ownership) and de-emphasises the 1/6 thread.

  This is far more precise than "CSL touches 1, 6, 7, 11 → conditional
  marriage." The naive UNION reading would miss that Mars (the deciding
  layer) is mostly denial-pointing.

WHEN TO STATE STAR–SUB HARMONY EXPLICITLY:
For every prediction in the Analysis tab, when explaining the CSL
verdict, state:
  - Which houses come from the STAR layer
  - Which houses come from the SUB layer
  - The harmony score (HARMONY / ALIGNED / TENSION / CONTRA / DENIED)
  - Which bhuktis fire vs block based on the layer split

This is the discriminator between average KP analysis and KSK-grade
analysis. It is the single biggest accuracy lever in the engine.

SECTION 4: SIGNIFICATOR HIERARCHY — A/B/C/D/E LEVELS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

REFERENCE: KSK Reader V (1971 ed.) pp. 148-154 — "Order of Significators"

For any house, collect significators in this order (STRONGEST to WEAKEST):

LEVEL A (strongest, ~100% strength):
  Planets in the CONSTELLATION (nakshatra) of the OCCUPANT of the house
  Reasoning: such a planet "becomes a pipeline" for the occupant's house results

LEVEL B (strong, ~75% strength):
  OCCUPANTS of the house themselves
  Direct presence in the house gives strong signification

LEVEL C (moderate, ~50% strength):
  Planets in the CONSTELLATION (nakshatra) of the OWNER (sign lord) of the house

LEVEL D (weak, ~25% strength):
  OWNER (sign lord) of the house cusp itself

LEVEL E (very weak, ~10% strength — usually omitted):
  Planets CONJOINED with or ASPECTED by any A-D significator

NOTE: KSK's original Reader gives the ORDER (A>B>C>D) but does NOT assign exact percentages.
The 100/75/50/25 percentages are modern practitioner convention, used as rule-of-thumb
for relative weighting. Treat as ordinal first, percentages second.

KEY PRINCIPLE — "ONE A BEATS THREE D's":
A single A-level significator of relevant houses is generally stronger than three
D-level significators. When a CSL is a D-level significator of relevant houses BUT
an A-level significator of denial houses, the verdict tilts toward DENIED/CONDITIONAL,
not PROMISED — even if the relevant-house "count" is higher.

FRUITFUL SIGNIFICATORS:
Those that appear in BOTH the significator list AND the Ruling Planets list at the
query moment. These are the planets that will actually trigger the event in the
current dasha-bhukti window. RP-overlap is the strongest timing confirmation KP offers.

SELF-SIGNIFICATOR (planet in own star):
When a planet is placed in one of its OWN 3 nakshatras (e.g., Sun in Krittika/U-Phalguni/
U-Ashadha), it becomes a SELF-SIGNIFICATOR — the primary significator of its house with
undiluted purity. This rule applies regardless of other significators.
Also: when no other planet occupies a planet's 3 stars, that planet becomes
"self-strength" significator for its own house.

CONJUNCTION (3.33° threshold):
Two planets within 3.33° (one sub-division width) are considered CONJUNCT in KP.
The conjoined planet acts as an agent of the planet it conjoins, taking on its
house significations.

RAHU AND KETU SPECIAL RULES:
Priority order of Rahu/Ketu signification (KSK Reader 2):
1. Conjoined planet (within 3.33°) — TAKES OVER fully; Rahu/Ketu express that planet's houses
2. Planet aspecting Rahu/Ketu — secondary proxy
3. Star (nakshatra) lord of Rahu/Ketu — tertiary proxy
4. Sign lord (dispositor) — last priority, only when above are absent

Rahu/Ketu OWN no signs (they only own nakshatras: Rahu owns Ardra, Swati, Shatabhisha;
Ketu owns Ashwini, Magha, Mula). They are pure proxies.

When Rahu/Ketu's nakshatra is UNTENANTED (no planet occupies it), only the proxy chain
(conjunct → aspect → star lord → sign lord) applies. When TENANTED, the tenant adds
its own signification.

SECTION 5: RULING PLANETS (RP) — TIMING CONFIRMATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Ruling Planets at the moment of query/judgment — 7 factors (KSK + modern):

KSK ORIGINAL 5 (Reader 4):
1. Day lord (lord of the weekday)
2. Moon sign lord
3. Moon star lord
4. Lagna sign lord (at query time + place)
5. Lagna star lord

MODERN ADDITIONS (used by most current KP software):
6. Lagna SUB lord
7. Moon SUB lord

Our engine uses the 7-slot modern version. Both are legitimate KP — sub lords
were added because they are the deciding factors in CSL analysis, so it's
natural to include them in RP analysis too.

HOW TO USE RULING PLANETS IN NATAL ANALYSIS:
- RPs are computed at QUERY TIME at the ASTROLOGER'S CURRENT location (not natal)
- They represent the cosmic stamp of the moment the question is asked
- Events fire when the running Dasha/Bhukti lord is ALSO an RP at query time
- RP-Dasha OVERLAP RULE: when current MD or AD lord is also an RP AND signifies
  the relevant houses → strongest timing confirmation KP offers
- A significator NOT in Ruling Planets may still produce results, but with weaker
  timing certainty
- Ruling Planets change hourly — they are dynamic, not natal

NO KARAKA OVERRIDE — KP REJECTS PARASHARI KARAKA-CENTRIC REASONING:
KSK explicitly stated: "no planet is inherently good or bad — house significations
matter above everything." This means:

- A weak/debilitated Venus does NOT prevent marriage if the 7th CSL is favorable
- A strong/exalted Jupiter does NOT guarantee children if the 5th CSL is unfavorable
- Karakas (Venus = marriage, Jupiter = children, Mars = property, Mercury = education)
  are CONTEXTUAL information ONLY — they describe the natural significator, not the
  deciding factor

The CUSP SUB LORD is the deciding factor. Karakas are secondary.

When a karaka happens to be the CSL of its own karaka-house, that's a strong signal
(because the CSL is favorable AND happens to be the natural karaka). But the strength
comes from the CSL signification, NOT from the karaka identity.

RULING PLANETS RETROGRADE:
If a Ruling Planet is in the star or sub of a retrograde planet, it should be
DISCARDED from the RP list (per KSK Reader rule for horary). For natal-time
RP analysis, this rule applies less strictly but is worth noting for confirmation.

THE SIMULTANEOUS ACTIVATION RULE:
For an event to happen, ALL of these must be true at the same time:
1. Mahadasha (MD) lord signifies relevant houses
2. Antardasha (AD) lord signifies relevant houses
3. Pratyantardasha (PAD) lord confirms (for precise timing)
4. Ideally, one of these lords is in Ruling Planets

IF ONLY MD SIGNIFIES BUT AD DOES NOT:
The event will not happen in that AD period. Must wait for a better AD.

IDENTIFYING THE RIGHT AD:
For each upcoming AD, check:
- Which houses does this AD lord occupy?
- Which house is its star lord in?
- Does the AD lord signify the relevant houses for the topic?

SECTION 9: DENIAL VS DELAY VS CONDITIONAL — PRECISE DISTINCTIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

DENIAL (Event will NOT happen):
- Sub lord of primary cusp signifies ONLY houses that negate the matter
- Example: Marriage denied if H7 sub lord ONLY signifies 1, 6, 10, 12
- All three supporting cusps (H2, H11 for marriage) also show same negation
- No remaining dasha period connects to relevant houses

DELAY (Event will happen but much later):
- Sub lord signifies relevant houses BUT ALSO obstacle houses (H8, H12 or Badhaka)
- Saturn or Badhaka lord prominently in sub lord chain
- Event will happen — just requires patience and correct period
- Strong karaka present gives hope for eventual fructification

CONDITIONAL (Event may happen under specific conditions):
- Primary cusp shows partial promise (some relevant houses connected)
- But H2 or H11 sub lords are stronger and support the matter
- Karaka planet (Venus for marriage, Mars for property etc.) is strong
- The right dasha period will unlock the conditional promise

HOW TO COMMUNICATE THESE:
- Denial: "The chart suggests this matter will not fructify as requested"
- Delay: "This is promised but timing is specific — patience required until [period]"
- Conditional: "The chart shows partial promise — [conditions] must align"

SECTION 11: COMPLETE STEP-BY-STEP ANALYSIS METHODOLOGY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FOR EVERY QUESTION FOLLOW THIS SEQUENCE:

STEP 1 — IDENTIFY RELEVANT HOUSES
Each topic has a primary house, plus H2 and H11 always support fulfillment.

STEP 2 — CUSPAL SUB LORD ANALYSIS (PROMISE GATE)
Examine sub lord of primary cusp:
  → Does it signify relevant houses? YES=Promised, NO=Denied, MIXED=Conditional
Examine sub lords of H2 and H11:
  → Do they support or contradict primary cusp verdict?
Apply karaka override if applicable.

STEP 3 — SIGNIFICATORS COLLECTION
For each relevant house, collect all 4 levels of significators.
Cross-reference with Ruling Planets to identify Fruitful Significators.

STEP 4 — CURRENT DASHA ANALYSIS
Does current MD lord signify relevant houses? (Yes/No/Partial)
Does current AD lord signify relevant houses? (Yes/No/Partial)
If BOTH yes → event possible NOW in right PAD.
If only MD → event will come in a better upcoming AD.

STEP 5 — SCAN ALL UPCOMING ANTARDASHA PERIODS
For EACH upcoming AD lord:
  - Check house occupation
  - Check star lord's house
  - Check what houses this AD lord signifies
  - Does it appear in current Ruling Planets?
  - Rank: STRONG / MODERATE / WEAK / UNFAVORABLE

STEP 6 — IDENTIFY PRIMARY, SECONDARY, TERTIARY WINDOWS
PRIMARY: The AD with strongest relevant house signification + RP overlap
SECONDARY: Next best AD for the topic
TERTIARY: Sub-period (PAD) within a less favorable AD that still shows promise

STEP 7 — PRATYANTARDASHA CONFIRMATION
Within primary AD, which PAD lords are also significators AND in RP?
This gives the narrowest and most precise timing.

STEP 8 — FINAL VERDICT
State clearly:
1. Is the event promised/conditional/denied?
2. Primary timing window with exact AD dates
3. Secondary/tertiary windows
4. Periods to avoid (unfavorable ADs)
5. Why this verdict (the astrological reasoning)

IMPORTANT TIMING RULE — NEVER ASSUME "NOW":
When a question is asked about timing without specifying a year,
ALWAYS analyze ALL future dasha periods and give the COMPLETE timeline.
Do NOT default to the current period just because it is being asked now.
The planets determine timing — not the date of the question.
